Graduate Student

Alyssa is a dual-degree master's student at the University of Michigan studying at the School of Information with a focus in Digital Curation and at the School for Environment and Sustainability with a focus in Geospatial Data Sciences.

Her research interests center around characterizing the built and natural environment to aid in community-based solutions for urban sustainability and climate resiliency. She is particularly interested in the access and distribution of greenspace and active transportation as well as the reflection of social ideologies on citizen decision making within urban space.

Identifying Subtextual Narratives in Pictorial Maps

Alyssa analyzed pictorial maps depicting the United States from the 1920-1960s to understand how they reflect and sustain political, economic, cultural, and social dynamics through symbology to identify subtextual narratives related to cultural caricaturization, capitalism and consumerism, American nationalism, enclosure and dispossession of land and exploitation of resources, and segregation in the depiction of labor and leisure activities.

Aiding in Civil Technology

Alyssa supported the development of a civic technology tool to aid in government transparency for residents of Dearborn, Michigan collaborating with the City of Dearborn's Department of Public Works.

Characterizing the Urban Tree Canopy

Alyssa's Master's Project at the School for Environment and Sustainability partnered with the City of Ann Arbor's Office of Sustainability and Innovations and Environmental Commission to capture the spatial distribution and composition of the urban tree canopy, identify turfgrass, delineate native forest fragments, and inform land stewardship practices.
