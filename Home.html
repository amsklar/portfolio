Alyssa is a dual-degree Graduate Student at the University of Michigan, where she focuses on the application of GIS and Remote Sensing techniques to community-based solutions for urban sustainability, public health, and climate resiliency. Additionally, she is interested in managing and preserving data to ensure its accessibility and longevity.

She is an Urban Geographer who concentrates on the social, environmental, and power structures of spaces and places.

Alyssa is a Reference Assistant at the Stephen S. Clark Library for maps, government information, and data where she applies domain knowledge to constructing metadata, providing research support, and curating themed open house events.

She is a passionate educator who believes in centering anti-racist and decolonizing pedagogy while fostering a community of care both in and outside the classroom.
